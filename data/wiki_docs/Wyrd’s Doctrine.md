# Wyrd’s Doctrine

These 5 principles rests in the heart of Wyrdosphere. Protect it. Preserve it. Run around wild holding it tightly in your hands /

**1. MAKE, DON'T FAKE**

- While they're optimizing for algorithms, we're creating for humans
- Every project starts with "why would anyone actually care about this?"
- Success Metric: When they screenshot it and send it to a friend with "wtf"
- Action: Reject briefs that don't matter; amplify the ones that do

**2. THINK LONGER, NOT LOUDER**

- The attention economy is bankrupt. We're investing in the memory economy
- They're fighting over seconds of attention; we're fighting for space in memory
- Success Metric: They remember it a week later without being retargeted
- Action: Every idea must pass the "will anyone remember this next month?" test

**3. MAXIMIZE THE WYRD, MINIMIZE THE BORING**

- Everyone's making content. We're making statements
- If it could've been made by anyone else, it shouldn't be made by us
- Success Metric: You know it's Wyrd before you see the logo
- Action: Every project must have at least one element that makes someone say "you can't do that"

**4. SEEK THE GOOD FIGHT**

- Not every brief is worth the battle
- We fight for the briefs worth winning, not just the ones easy to win
- Success Metric: The work makes both us and the client uncomfortable (in a good way)
- Action: Actively pursue projects that challenge the status quo of their category

**5. CREATE GRAVITY, NOT JUST MOTION**

- While they're chasing trends, we're creating them
- Don't just join conversations – start them
- Success Metric: Others start copying elements of what we do
- Action: Every project should establish a new standard for what's possible
