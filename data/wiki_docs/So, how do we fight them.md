# So, how do we fight them?

### **PHASE 1: THE SCOUT**

- **Objective:** Identify the briefs worth fighting for
- **Tactics:**
    - Ask the questions no one else is asking
    - Look for clients who are uncomfortable with the obvious
    - Seek out the problems, not just the symptoms
- **Success Looks Like:** Walking away from wrong opportunities faster than anyone else

### **PHASE 2: THE SPARK**

- **Objective:** Turn briefs into beacons
- **Tactics:**
    - Find the tension no one else sees
    - Convert problems into provocations
    - Make the impossible seem inevitable
- **Success Looks Like:** The brief becomes unrecognizable in the best possible way

### **PHASE 3: THE STORM**

- **Objective:** Execute with controlled chaos
- **Tactics:**
    - Break every rule that doesn't serve the idea
    - Follow every rule that does
    - Technical excellence in service of creative rebellion
- **Success Looks Like:** Work that makes people uncomfortable before it makes them nod

### **PHASE 4: THE SHOCK**

- **Objective:** Launch with maximum impact
- **Tactics:**
    - Never just release – detonate
    - Design for the screenshot, optimize for the DM
    - Make the launch itself as wyrd as the work
- **Success Looks Like:** People sharing it not because they like it, but because they can't not

### **PHASE 5: THE STORY**

- **Objective:** Turn projects into legends
- **Tactics:**
    - Document the battles, not just the victories
    - Tell the stories behind the work that make it matter more
    - Make the case studies as wyrd as the work itself
- **Success Looks Like:** The stories of how it happened spread faster than the work itself
